A Case Study In the Jorhat distict of Assam,India

Introduction: At present, about 217 million children, all over the world work as labourer. The presence of such a large number of child labourers is regarded as a serious issue in terms of economic welfare. Children who work fail to get necessary education. They do not get the opportunity to develop physically, intellectually, emotionally and psychologically. Hence it is an urgent need of the hour to analyse the matter, thoroughly, so that effective attempts could be made for eradication of the problem. Thorough analysis requires micro level study since the scenario of child labour may vary place to place, time to time, state to state and country to country so also their addressing policy. The present work is an attempt in this regard considering Jorhat district of Assam, India, as locale of the study.

Methodology:

(a) Study area and population- Keeping in view the concentration of child labour in both rural and urban sector required data were collected from 3 Development Blocks and 5 (five) Municipality Wards of the district.. As per census 2001, total no of child labour in Jorhat district (5-14 years) is 16231. The sample size of the study was 120 respondents. (b) Study design- Both qualitative and quantitative methods were applied in the study. Qualitative data were collected through well structured interview schedule and by carrying out observations. Quantitative data were collected by using a multistage sampling method to identify or select the study participants (respondents).
